Lessons Learned from My First Freelance Project

What I wish I knew before starting as a freelance designer and developer

📌 1. Clear Communication is Everything

I learned quickly that **unclear instructions lead to confusion**. My first client gave vague feedback like “make it pop” or “it feels off.” I had to learn to ask better questions like:

  • What is the goal of this feature?
  • Do you have a reference site or example in mind?
  • Can you describe what “off” means to you?

🧾 2. Never Start Without a Contract

I started without a formal agreement—bad move. Midway through, the client changed their mind about the scope. Now, I always use a basic contract outlining:

  • Project scope & deliverables
  • Deadlines
  • Payment terms
  • Revision limits

⏱ 3. Time Tracking Saves You

I underestimated how long things took. By tracking my time using apps like Toggle or Clockify, I could improve my pricing and avoid undercharging.

💸 4. Ask for a Deposit

On my first project, I waited until the end to get paid—and waited weeks. Now I request **50% upfront** and the rest on completion. It protects both sides.

📂 5. Keep Everything Organized

I used to lose client files or forget where I saved design drafts. I switched to organized folders like:

/ClientName/
  /Designs/
  /Development/
  /Invoices/

🎯 6. Focus on Solving Problems, Not Just Making Things Pretty

As a beginner, I focused on beautiful design. But clients care more about results. I started asking, “How will this help your users?” or “What’s the goal of this section?”

🔚 Final Thoughts

Freelancing is exciting but challenging. I made a lot of beginner mistakes, but each one taught me something valuable. If you’re starting out, take the time to learn from others—and from yourself.
